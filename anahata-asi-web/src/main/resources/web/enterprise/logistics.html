Systems of Action over Systems of Record

In 2026, visibility without action is just informing your company that it is failing in real-time. Anahata ASI goes beyond passive analytics dashboards, deploying autonomous agents directly inside your local JVM to securely integrate with legacy ERPs (SAP, Oracle) via JDBC and execute automated inventory rebalancing under strict human auditing.

SUPPLY CHAIN SPECIFICATIONS

Legacy Database Bridges

Query and update local, firewalled ERP and WMS databases on-premises over secure, transaction-grade JDBC/JMS networks without external internet tunnels.

Local Open-Weights

Runs offline open-weight models (like Llama 4 Scout, DeepSeek V4 Pro, and GLM-5.1) over a secure localhost loopback, protecting proprietary operational workflows.

Human-In-The-Loop (HITL)

Every automated purchase order, re-routing request, or stock transfer is blocked in a PENDING state for operator review before execution, preventing errant orders.

Immutable Transaction Logs

Kryo state passivation serializes history, resources, and tool variables directly to a secure, unalterable flight recorder ledger on every single turn for DORA audit compliance.

Intranet Isolation & Security

Modern supply chain logistics require deep security to protect warehouse topology, carrier contracts, and trade routes. Anahata ASI can operate completely locally. By hosting open-weights on your secure corporate networks, operational data is protected by physical firewalls with zero external internet telemetry.

Self-Correcting Warehouse Management

Traditional RPA (Robotic Process Automation) is fragile and breaks when a process deviates. Anahata ASI enables true Agentic Process Automation (APA). When anomalies occur (such as logistics bottlenecks, late carrier arrivals, or stock shortfalls), the agent reasons through the goal, proposes an adaptive re-routing or slotting plan, and presents the action for human review.

This ensures your warehouse operations remain flexible, resilient, and fully operational without risking un-supervised automated failures.
